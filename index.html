CHAPTER 1 — THEORY

IGA Fundamentals

Before touching OneIM, you must understand the field it operates in. Identity Governance and Administration (IGA) is a discipline — OneIM is its implementation.

🟢 Beginner — Essential Foundation

The Core Problem IGA Solves

In every organization, people join, move between departments, and eventually leave. Each person needs access to systems — email, file shares, business applications, HR systems, AD accounts, etc.

⚠️
Access Sprawl
Employees accumulate more access than needed over time. When they move departments, old access is rarely removed — violating least-privilege principle.
🔍
No Visibility
IT can't answer "who has access to what?" — a basic question that becomes impossible without centralized tracking across dozens of systems.
📋
Compliance Risk
Regulations like SOX, GDPR, HIPAA require demonstrable control over who accessed sensitive data. Manual systems cannot produce this audit trail reliably.

IGA is the discipline that solves these problems — by providing a central platform to govern identities, manage access lifecycle, enforce policies, and produce compliance evidence.

Key Terminology — Know These Cold

TermDefinitionOneIM Relevance
IdentityA digital representation of a real person in the system. Separate from their accounts.The Person table in OneIM.
AccountA specific login credential on a target system (e.g., an AD user account, an SAP user).ADSAccount, SAPUser tables.
EntitlementA specific permission or access right — a group membership, a role, a license, a permission set.AD groups, Business Roles, IT Shop services.
ProvisioningThe process of creating or modifying access in a target system based on a business rule or request.Job Server executing write-back tasks to AD, SAP.
De-provisioningRemoving or disabling access when no longer needed (e.g., person leaves the company).Triggered by HR-driven events, disables accounts and roles.
SynchronizationReading data FROM a target system INTO the IGA platform's database to maintain accurate records.Synchronization Editor + sync projects.
AttestationA review process where resource owners or managers certify that access assignments are still correct and necessary.Attestation module in OneIM.
SoDSegregation of Duties — a control that prevents one person from holding conflicting access rights (e.g., creating AND approving payments).Compliance & Attestation section.
RBACRole-Based Access Control — access is granted through roles, not directly to individuals. Easier to manage at scale.Business Roles in OneIM are RBAC-based.
ABACAttribute-Based Access Control — access decisions are based on attributes (department, location, title). More flexible than RBAC.Dynamic roles and IT Shop conditions.
Joiner ProcessThe workflow triggered when a new employee joins — creates accounts and assigns initial access based on job function.HR import triggers Person creation.
Mover ProcessThe workflow triggered when an employee changes roles — grants new access, removes old access that no longer applies.Department/position changes trigger recalculation.

IGA vs IAM — What's the Difference?

IAM — Identity & Access Management
  • Focus: Authentication and authorization (who are you, what can you do right now)
  • Tools: Active Directory, Azure AD, Okta, LDAP, SSO solutions
  • Question it answers: "Can this user log in? Can they access this resource?"
  • Time horizon: Real-time, transactional
  • Primary users: IT infrastructure teams
  • Examples: SSO, MFA, Active Directory
IGA — Identity Governance & Administration
  • Focus: Governance, lifecycle, compliance, visibility over access
  • Tools: OneIM, SailPoint, Saviynt, IBM ISIM
  • Question it answers: "Should this user have this access? Who approved it? Is it still needed?"
  • Time horizon: Lifecycle-oriented, process-driven
  • Primary users: IT security, audit, HR, compliance teams
  • Examples: OneIM, access reviews, provisioning workflows
💡
Key insight: OneIM is IGA, but it integrates WITH IAM systems (like Active Directory). OneIM reads from AD (synchronization) and writes back to AD (provisioning) — AD is a target system for OneIM.

The Identity Lifecycle — Joiner, Mover, Leaver

The most important concept in IGA is the identity lifecycle. Every identity goes through these stages:

  1. J
    Joiner — New Employee Arrives
    HR creates a record in the HRIS. OneIM reads this (via HR connector or CSV import). A Person record is created. Based on the person's department, job title, and location, OneIM automatically calculates which roles they should have. Accounts are provisioned to all connected systems.
  2. M
    Mover — Employee Changes Role
    HR updates the employee's department or job title. OneIM detects the change. New role entitlements are calculated and provisioned. Old entitlements that don't apply to the new role are revoked — all automatically and with a full audit trail.
  3. L
    Leaver — Employee Departs
    HR marks the employee as terminated. OneIM immediately — or on the effective date — disables all accounts across all connected target systems, removes all group memberships, and marks the Person as inactive. No more manual "can you remove this user from everything?" emails.
CHAPTER 2 — PLATFORM

What is One Identity Manager?

One Identity Manager (OneIM) is Quest Software's enterprise IGA platform. It is one of the leading IGA solutions in the market alongside SailPoint IdentityIQ and Saviynt.

🟢 Beginner — Essential Foundation

Platform Overview

OneIM is a modular, database-centric IGA platform that manages identity lifecycle, automated provisioning, access request workflows, compliance, and governance across the enterprise.

Unlike cloud-only SaaS IGA tools, OneIM runs in your infrastructure — giving you full control, deep customizability, and the ability to handle extremely complex enterprise requirements.

🔐
Identity Governance
Manage who has access to what. Enforce least-privilege. Run access certifications and attestation campaigns. Satisfy audit requirements.
⚡
Automated Provisioning
Automatically create, modify, or disable accounts in AD, Exchange, SAP, LDAP, Azure AD, cloud apps — driven by HR data and business rules.
🛒
IT Shop / Self-Service
Employees request access through a web portal. Approvals route automatically. Requests are tracked, auditable, and can be revoked on schedule.
📋
Compliance & Audit
Segregation of Duties rules, attestation campaigns, audit reports for SOX, GDPR, HIPAA, ISO 27001, and other regulatory frameworks.
🔗
200+ Connectors
Active Directory, Azure AD, Exchange, SAP, LDAP, Google Workspace, ServiceNow, SCIM, Oracle, Notes/Domino, and many more.
🏗️
Fully Customizable
VB.NET scripting, custom business logic, schema extensions, workflow customization — bend OneIM to your exact business requirements.

What is IAM and IGA?

Two critical but different disciplines. IAM (Identity & Access Management) handles authentication. IGA (Identity Governance & Administration) handles compliance and lifecycle. Let's understand both.

Real-World Scenario

📖
Imagine a bank with 10,000 employees. Sarah joins as a teller. After 3 years, she's promoted to branch manager. Then after 5 years, she's laid off during a downturn.

Questions that arise:
  • ✓ Can Sarah log into the system? (IAM)
  • ✓ Should Sarah have access to customer account info? (IGA)
  • ✓ When she was promoted, did we remove teller access? (IGA)
  • ✓ When she was laid off, was ALL her access disabled immediately? (IGA)
  • ✓ Can we prove to auditors that everything was done correctly? (IGA)

IAM — Identity & Access Management

IAM is about authentication and immediate access decisions. It answers: "Can you log in right now? Can you access this resource right now?"

AspectDetails
Primary FunctionAuthentication (proving who you are) and authorization (deciding what you can do)
Real-Time DecisionWhen you try to log in or access something RIGHT NOW, IAM systems check: "Is this allowed?"
ToolsActive Directory, Azure AD, Okta, Google Workspace, LDAP servers
Time HorizonImmediate (seconds, minutes) - what you can do RIGHT NOW
Who Uses ItIT infrastructure teams, system administrators
ExampleYou swipe your ID card at the building entrance. The reader checks if your card is valid (IAM says yes/no)

IGA — Identity Governance & Administration

IGA is about managing who SHOULD have access and proving you did it correctly. It answers: "Should this person have this access? Is it still needed? Can we audit everything?"

AspectDetails
Primary FunctionGovernance (deciding who should have what), lifecycle management (changes), and compliance (proving it was correct)
Business ProcessWhen HR says someone transferred departments, IGA automatically recalculates what access they should have
ToolsOneIM, SailPoint, Saviynt, IBM ISIM
Time HorizonLong-term (days, weeks, months) - decisions based on business rules and lifecycle events
Who Uses ItIT security, compliance teams, HR, internal audit, executive management
ExampleYou transfer to a new department. A process automatically grants you access to the new department's systems, removes the old ones, logs everything for auditors

How IAM and IGA Work Together

IAM (The Lock & Key)
  • Question: Can you enter right now?
  • Decision Speed: Milliseconds
  • Action: Grant or deny login/access
  • Example: Authentication server checks your password
IGA (The Decision Maker)
  • Question: Should they have access?
  • Decision Speed: Hours/days (overnight jobs)
  • Action: Add/remove access based on business rules
  • Example: Role-based access recalculation job runs nightly
✅
Key Relationship: IGA decides what access SHOULD exist. Then it tells IAM systems (like Active Directory) to set it up. IAM enforces those decisions at login time.

Why OneIM Matters

In large organizations, managing access manually creates risk, waste, and compliance violations. OneIM solves these problems at enterprise scale.

The Problem Without OneIM

Imagine you're the IT manager at a 1,000-person company without an IGA tool:

⚠️
Access Sprawl
The Problem: Sarah started 10 years ago as a junior analyst. Over time, different departments gave her access to different systems. She got promoted 3 times. Nobody removed her old access. She now has access to systems she doesn't need.

Risk: If her account is hacked, attackers can access all those systems.
🔍
No Visibility
The Problem: Your CEO asks: "Who has access to financial records?" You check Active Directory... then three different databases... then several SaaS apps... then spreadsheets. You can't find a complete answer in under a week.

Risk: You can't control what you can't see. Auditors will fail you.
⚙️
Manual Processes
The Problem: When someone joins, you manually fill out forms, email requests to different departments, wait for approvals, manually create accounts in 5 different systems.

Risk: Takes 2-3 weeks. Mistakes happen. Some systems don't get configured. Costs $1,000+ per hire in IT time.
📋
Compliance Risk
The Problem: Your company is audited for SOX/GDPR/HIPAA compliance. Auditors ask: "Can you prove who had access to sensitive data in January?" You have no central record.

Risk: Failed audit. Fines up to millions of dollars.
🕐
Slow Offboarding
The Problem: John leaves the company. Theoretically you should disable his account that day. But you have 6 different systems. You remember 3 of them. The other 3... John still has access months later.

Risk: A disgruntled ex-employee can still access company data.
💼
Manual Reviews
The Problem: Every year, you have to do access reviews. You send an email to 1,000 managers: "Verify that these 100 people should have system access." Response rate: 10%. Data quality: poor.

Risk: Inappropriate access goes undetected. Auditors reject your access certification process.

The Solution: OneIM

OneIM automates everything:

✅
Automatic Provisioning
John joins the company. HR enters his record in their system. OneIM automatically creates accounts in AD, Exchange, Salesforce, Workday, and 3 other systems. Takes seconds instead of days.
✅
Automatic De-provisioning
Sarah leaves. OneIM automatically disables her account in ALL systems the same day. No forgotten systems. No lingering access.
✅
Central Visibility
CEO asks: "Who has financial access?" OneIM answers in seconds. Complete audit trail showing exactly when it was granted and why.
✅
Lifecycle Management
When Mike transfers from Sales to Support, OneIM automatically calculates: remove Sales roles, add Support roles. All audited and tracked.
✅
Compliance Automation
Access reviews? OneIM sends them automatically to the right managers. Results are tracked. Compliance reports are generated with audit trails proving everything was correct.

IGA Fundamentals — Core Concepts Explained

The building blocks of identity governance. Understand these and you understand OneIM at its core.

The 5 Core Concepts

👤
Identity (Person)
The Human Behind the Accounts

What is it? A digital representation of a real person in the system. ONE person, ONE identity record.

Example: Sarah Chen is ONE identity. But she might have accounts in 10 different systems (AD, Exchange, Salesforce, Workday, SAP, etc.). Those are all tied to her ONE identity.

In OneIM: Stored in the Person table with attributes like name, employee ID, department, manager, start date, end date.

Why it matters: Without a central identity, you'd have to manage 10 separate records for 1 person. Chaos.

🔐
Account
Login Credentials on a System

What is it? A specific login credential on a target system. Many accounts can point to ONE identity.

Example: Sarah's AD account is schenuate. Her SAP account is SAP_CHEN. Her Salesforce username is sarah.chen@mycompany.com. All three accounts represent ONE identity (Sarah).

In OneIM: Account records track which system, what username, what status (active/disabled), account creation date, and link back to the Person identity.

Why it matters: When OneIM knows all of Sarah's accounts, it can manage them all together. When she leaves, OneIM can disable all of them automatically.

📊
Entitlement
A Permission or Access Right

What is it? A specific permission, group membership, or role that grants access to something.

Examples:

  • • Group membership: FinanceTeam AD group (grants access to finance share drives)
  • • Role: Manager role in Salesforce (grants permission to approve deals)
  • • Permission: Edit Reports in analytics tool
  • • License: Office 365 license with Teams, OneDrive, SharePoint

In OneIM: Entitlements can be managed as Business Roles (OneIM's own role system) or synced from target systems (AD groups).

Why it matters: IGA is about deciding WHO should have WHICH entitlements. OneIM enforces those decisions.

➡️
Provisioning
Granting Access

What is it? The automatic or manual process of creating or modifying access in target systems based on a decision.

Flow: OneIM decides → OneIM calls system → System creates/updates the access

Example: OneIM decides Sarah should be in the FinanceTeam AD group. OneIM connects to AD and adds her account to that group. Done. No manual intervention.

In OneIM: Job Server handles provisioning. It connects to each target system (AD, SAP, Salesforce, etc.) and makes changes.

Why it matters: Manual provisioning is slow and error-prone. Automatic provisioning is fast and reliable.

❌
De-provisioning
Removing Access

What is it? Removing access when it's no longer needed.

When it happens:

  • • Employee leaves (immediate or future date)
  • • Employee transfers departments (old access removed)
  • • Access review (manager certifies this person doesn't need this anymore)
  • • Policy change (SoD rules say this role conflicts with that role)

Example: Sarah leaves the company on Friday. OneIM automatically disables her AD account, removes her from all Office 365 groups, disables her SAP access, marks her in Salesforce as inactive. All automatic. All audited.

In OneIM: Triggered by Person status changes, role removals, or scheduled jobs.

Why it matters: If access isn't removed, ex-employees can still access company data. Security nightmare.

Putting It Together

🔄
The IGA Cycle:

1. Identity Created: Sarah joins company. HR system creates a record. OneIM reads it and creates a Person object.

2. Entitlements Assigned: Based on her role (Financial Analyst), OneIM calculates: she should have access to Finance department resources, Financial reports role, and Accounting systems.

3. Provisioning Happens: OneIM automatically creates/updates accounts in AD, SAP, Workday, Finance portal. Minutes, not days.

4. Sarah Works: For 3 years, OneIM tracks all her access. Managers certify it's still needed during annual reviews.

5. Transfer Happens: Sarah moves to Operations. OneIM recalculates: remove Finance roles, add Operations roles. Automatic provisioning/de-provisioning happens.

6. Leaver Process: Sarah retires. OneIM marks her as inactive. All accounts disabled across all systems. Audit trail created proving it was all correct.

The Identity Lifecycle: Joiner-Mover-Leaver

Every person goes through three stages in their employment. OneIM automates the process at each stage.

The Three Stages

  1. J
    JOINER — Employee Starts
    The Scenario: Your company hired Sarah Chen. Her first day is Monday.

    Without OneIM (Manual Process):
    • Friday before: IT manager gets email from HR saying new hire
    • IT manually creates AD account, Exchange mailbox, adds to groups
    • Calls SAP team to create SAP user (takes 2-3 days)
    • Calls Salesforce admin to set up access (another day)
    • Sets up phone, laptop, building access manually
    • Total time: 5-10 days. Sarah's first week is wasted waiting for access

    With OneIM (Automated Process):
    • HR enters Sarah into HRIS system Friday morning
    • OneIM picks up the new hire event automatically
    • OneIM reads Sarah's role: "Financial Analyst"
    • OneIM calculates: she needs Finance department access, Financial role, AD groups, SAP module, Salesforce access
    • Within 15 minutes, OneIM provisions everything across all systems
    • Sarah logs in Monday morning. Everything is ready
    • Total time: 15 minutes automatic. Sarah is productive day one

    What OneIM Actually Does:
    1. 1. Creates Person record with Sarah's info
    2. 2. Assigns her Business Role based on job title
    3. 3. Provisioning job runs: creates AD account, mailbox, SAP user, Salesforce user
    4. 4. Adds her to appropriate AD groups (Finance, Users, Department-specific)
    5. 5. Sets manager, cost center, organizational hierarchy
    6. 6. Logs everything: who, what, when, why (for audits)
  2. M
    MOVER — Employee Transfers/Promotes
    The Scenario: After 2 years, Sarah gets promoted from Financial Analyst to Finance Manager.

    Without OneIM (Manual Process):
    • HR sends email to IT: "Sarah transferred to Manager role"
    • IT manually adds her to Manager groups in AD
    • IT manually removes her from Analyst groups (usually forgotten)
    • IT calls SAP team: "Give her manager access" (2-3 days)
    • Salesforce: "Manager role needed" (another day)
    • Nobody checks if this violates SoD rules
    • Result: Sarah has both old AND new access (sprawl) for weeks or months

    With OneIM (Automated Process):
    • HR updates Sarah's role in HRIS: "Finance Manager"
    • OneIM detects the change automatically
    • OneIM recalculates entitlements: remove Analyst roles, add Manager roles
    • OneIM checks SoD rules: does Manager + any other role create conflicts? (e.g., can't approve AND receive payments)
    • Within 15 minutes, provisioning happens: removes old groups, adds new groups, updates SAP and Salesforce
    • Audit trail shows exactly what was removed and when

    What OneIM Actually Does:
    1. 1. Detects role change via HR integration
    2. 2. Retrieves Business Role definition for "Finance Manager"
    3. 3. Compares current entitlements vs. new entitlements
    4. 4. Plans: These groups to ADD, These groups to REMOVE
    5. 5. Checks SoD rules: are there policy violations?
    6. 6. De-provisions old access from all systems
    7. 7. Provisions new access to all systems
    8. 8. Updates Person record, creates audit log
  3. L
    LEAVER — Employee Departs
    The Scenario: After 5 years, Sarah gets a job offer elsewhere and leaves the company. Her last day is Friday.

    Without OneIM (Manual Process):
    • HR notifies IT on Thursday: "Sarah leaves Friday"
    • IT manager tries to remember all systems (AD, Exchange, Salesforce, SAP, Workday, ...)
    • Manually disables AD account Friday afternoon
    • Exchange mailbox: forgotten (IT manager went home)
    • Salesforce: someone forgets to disable
    • SAP access: disabled Monday (another team)
    • VPN access: nobody remembers until IT audit 6 months later
    • Result: Sarah still has access to multiple systems 6 months after leaving

    With OneIM (Automated Process):
    • HR marks Sarah as "Leaver" with end date Friday
    • OneIM is configured to de-provision on end date
    • Friday morning (end date), OneIM checks: Sarah is marked as terminated
    • OneIM immediately de-provisions across ALL systems simultaneously
    • Within 5 minutes: AD account disabled, Exchange deleted, Salesforce marked inactive, SAP locked, VPN revoked, etc.
    • Complete audit trail showing exactly when access was removed from each system

    What OneIM Actually Does:
    1. 1. Detects end-of-employment date via HR integration
    2. 2. On end date (or configured offboarding date), initiates de-provisioning
    3. 3. De-provisioning job connects to ALL target systems
    4. 4. Removes/disables Sarah from: AD groups, AD account status, Exchange, Salesforce, SAP, Workday, VPN, etc.
    5. 5. Updates Person status to "Inactive"
    6. 6. Creates complete audit trail proving all access was removed
    7. 7. Sends notification to managers/compliance team

RBAC, Business Roles & Access Control

How OneIM decides who gets what access. The core concept is called Role-Based Access Control (RBAC), and it's simpler than you think.

RBAC — Role-Based Access Control

Core Idea: Instead of managing access for individual people, you manage access for ROLES. Then assign people to roles.

Why RBAC?

❌ Bad Way: Individual Access
  • Sarah → Finance group
  • Sarah → Reports role
  • Sarah → Accounting module
  • Sarah → Cost center 12345
  • ...(repeated for each person)
  • Problem: 1000 people = 5000+ individual decisions. Impossible to manage.
✅ Good Way: Role-Based Access
  • Define Financial Analyst Role ONCE:
  • → Finance group
  • → Reports role
  • → Accounting module
  • → Cost center 12345
  • Then assign:
  • Sarah → Financial Analyst role
  • Mike → Financial Analyst role
  • Jessica → Financial Analyst role
  • Benefit: Define once, reuse 100 times. Change once, affects all 100 people.

Business Roles in OneIM

OneIM has a concept called "Business Roles" (also called "Dynamic Roles" in some systems). These are OneIM's way of implementing RBAC.

💡
Business Role = A template of entitlements

Define a role once. Assign it to people. OneIM calculates what systems they should access. Automatically provisions that access.
📋
Financial Analyst Role
OneIM Business Role Example

Definition:

Role Name: Financial Analyst
Description: Full access to finance systems for staff-level analysts

Entitlements:
  • AD Group: Finance_Users
  • AD Group: Finance_Reports
  • SAP Role: FIN_ANALYST
  • SAP Role: GL_ACCESS
  • Salesforce Permission: Financial Data Access
  • Workday Role: Finance (read-only)

What Happens:

  • • Assign Sarah to this role → OneIM provisions ALL these entitlements
  • • Assign Mike to this role → OneIM provisions ALL these entitlements
  • • Change the role definition (add: Cost Center Budget Access) → ALL people with this role immediately get the new entitlement
  • • Remove Sarah from this role → OneIM de-provisions ALL these entitlements

Role Hierarchy

Roles can be organized hierarchically (parent-child relationships) to avoid duplicating common entitlements.

🌳
Example Role Hierarchy:
Finance Employee (Base)
├─ AD Group: Company_Users
├─ AD Group: Finance
├─ Office 365 License
│
├── Financial Analyst (extends Finance Employee)
│ ├─ AD Group: Finance_Reports
│ ├─ SAP Role: FIN_ANALYST
│ └─ Salesforce: Financial Data
│
├── Finance Manager (extends Finance Employee)
│ ├─ AD Group: Finance_Managers
│ ├─ SAP Role: FIN_MANAGER
│ ├─ Approval permission for expenses
│ └─ Workday: Finance Manager Dashboard
│
└── Finance Director (extends Finance Manager)
├─ AD Group: Finance_Leadership
├─ SAP Role: FIN_DIRECTOR
├─ Budget approval authority
└─ Executive dashboards

Each role inherits from parent, so you don't duplicate "Finance Employee" entitlements.

Role Assignment Rules

OneIM can calculate role assignments based on IF/THEN rules:

⚙️
Example Rule Set for Financial Analyst Role:

IF (Department = "Finance" OR Department = "Accounting")
AND (JobTitle = "Analyst" OR JobTitle = "Senior Analyst")
AND EmploymentStatus = "Active"
THEN Assign "Financial Analyst" role

What this means: Everyone in Finance/Accounting departments with Analyst job titles automatically gets the Financial Analyst role. If they leave or change departments, the role is automatically removed.
